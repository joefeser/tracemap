Claim review drill

Practice the proof check before repeating a claim.

This short drill gives readers authored public-safe sample claims, proof fields to inspect, and an answer key for bounded reuse.

Public claim level: concept. No public conclusion without evidence. This is a learning exercise, not an automated grader, approval workflow, release gate, runtime proof, or replacement for human review.

Drill setup

Read the claim, then inspect the evidence fields.

What to practiceDecide whether the sentence can be repeated, downgraded, held for owner follow-up, kept internal, or not repeated.
What stays outside the drillThe answer key does not prove software behavior, approve releases, score claims, or certify coverage.
What counts as supportUse only public-safe proof paths, rule IDs or rule families, evidence tiers, coverage labels, limitations, non-claims, source context, and public/private status.

Evidence checklist

Inspect the same fields every time.

proof pathPublic-safe page, summary, report, sanctioned demo artifact, rule catalog page, or review-packet surface.
rule ID or rule familySpecific public-safe rule ID when available; otherwise a rule family with its limitation.
evidence tierOne of Tier1Semantic, Tier2Structural, Tier3SyntaxOrTextual, or Tier4Unknown.
coverage labelTranscribed from the cited support, including reduced, partial, unknown, unavailable, or future-only labels.
limitationWhat the evidence cannot support.
non-claimWhat the reader must not infer from the row.
source contextMain, checked-in public demo, public documentation, future-only, hidden, or local-only status.
public/private statusWhether the support can be cited publicly or must stay internal.

Unsafe answer examples

Rejected patterns are not TraceMap claims.

  • Do not answer that a static row proves runtime behavior, production traffic, endpoint performance, or outage cause.
  • Do not answer that a checklist approves a release, proves release safety, proves operational safety, or replaces release controls.
  • Do not answer that missing evidence is filled by confidence, seniority, repetition, or schedule pressure.
  • Do not answer that private-only support can be cited publicly before a reviewed summary exists.
  • Do not answer that the drill is AI/LLM analysis, autonomous grading, or a substitute for human review.

Stop conditions

Stop when the evidence boundary breaks.

No proof pathDo not repeat the claim until a public-safe support path exists.
No rule ID or rule familyAsk for owner follow-up rather than inventing a rule.
Missing tier or coverage labelKeep the claim internal or downgrade it until the evidence strength and coverage state are visible.
Private-only supportUse internal follow-up until the owner provides a public-safe summary.
Runtime, release, or safety wordingRoute those questions to the responsible owners and remove the public TraceMap claim.

Non-claims

The drill teaches review discipline, not new proof.

  • This page does not publish raw facts, raw SQLite content, analyzer logs, raw source snippets, raw SQL, config values, secrets, local paths, raw remotes, generated scan directories, private sample names, raw command output, hidden validation details, or credential-like values.
  • The drill does not prove runtime behavior, production traffic, endpoint performance, outage cause, release approval, release safety, operational safety, absence of impact, complete coverage, AI impact analysis, LLM analysis, or replacement of human review.
  • A correct answer is a reuse decision for the sample sentence, not proof that the underlying software is shipped, demo-backed, safe, approved, complete, or impact-free.
