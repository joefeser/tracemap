Evidence review meeting agenda

Run the meeting from the question to the handoff.

This agenda gives engineers, reviewers, architects, managers, release reviewers, and owners a compact script for inspecting deterministic static evidence before repeating a public-facing conclusion.

Public claim level: concept. No public conclusion without evidence. The agenda is a human meeting aid for static evidence review, not meeting automation, release approval, runtime proof, production traffic proof, endpoint performance proof, absence-of-impact proof, complete coverage, AI analysis, LLM analysis, or a replacement for human judgment or governance.

Before the meeting

Start with the question before any claim is repeated.

Frame the questionName the bounded review question, the repeated wording under review, and the audience that needs the answer.
Prepare the proof pathBring public-safe proof paths, or mark the proof path as missing before discussion begins.
Bring evidence metadataAttach rule ID or rule family, evidence tier, coverage label, limitation, and public-safe provenance when available.
Pre-mark gapsPrivate-only, unavailable, unknown, reduced, or syntax-only material starts as a gap, not as support for stronger wording.

Agenda

Eight rows keep the review attached to evidence.

Required meeting rows. Each row carries a purpose, evidence input, and stop or handoff output so unsupported wording is removed, downgraded, or assigned before notes are shared.
Agenda row Meeting purpose Evidence input Stop or handoff output
question framing State the review question before any claim is repeated. Bounded question, intended audience, and draft wording under review. One question, plus wording to remove, downgrade, or route to another owner.
proof path check Confirm where public-safe evidence can be inspected. Candidate proof-path links, public-safe provenance, or private-review status. Proof path link, private-review note, or missing-proof gap.
evidence tier/coverage check Pair the cited tier with the coverage label. Tier1Semantic, Tier2Structural, Tier3SyntaxOrTextual, or Tier4Unknown with a coverage label. Tier and coverage label retained without upgrade.
limitation check Attach what the static evidence cannot prove. Rule ID or rule family, limitation note, coverage label, and evidence tier. Limitation kept beside the meeting note or public wording.
gap register check Capture unknown, reduced, unavailable, or private-only evidence. Gap state, review question, proof route status, and owner type. Gap entry with question, limitation, and next owner.
owner follow-up Assign the next owner for unresolved review work. Open gaps, unresolved questions, and needed evidence category. Role-based owner and requested follow-up.
decision record Prepare bounded handoff notes. Question, evidence state, validation evidence category, limitations, gaps, owners, and non-claims. Decision-record ingredients preserved with limitations and without stronger claims.
closeout Review notes before they leave the room. Draft notes, unsupported wording, open gaps, and owner assignments. Unsupported claims removed, downgraded, or assigned to a non-static owner.

Evidence checks

Do not repeat evidence until the rule and limitation travel with it.

proof pathPresent, missing, private-review only, or unavailable.
rule ID or rule familyRequired before evidence is repeated as meeting support.
evidence tierUse only Tier1Semantic, Tier2Structural, Tier3SyntaxOrTextual, or Tier4Unknown.
coverage labelCarry reduced, unknown, unavailable, syntax-only, or private-only coverage into the note.
limitationState what static evidence cannot answer and point to validation and limitations when needed.

Gap capture

Gaps become follow-up rows, not quiet upgrades.

Record the missing proofUse the gap register pattern when a proof path, validation evidence category, or public-safe summary is absent.
Name the evidence stateReduced, unknown, unavailable, syntax-only, and private-only coverage stays visible.
Keep wording boundedUnsupported runtime, release, safety, production, performance, absence-of-impact, coverage, AI/LLM, or governance wording is moved into a stop condition.

Owner assignment

Assign the next question to a role, not a private name.

source ownerReviews code meaning, contract shape, or framework context outside public copy.
test ownerSupplies test or validation evidence when static evidence is insufficient.
runtime ownerHandles observability, traffic, and operational questions outside the static meeting note.
release reviewerHandles release-process decisions outside TraceMap's static evidence boundary.
evidence ownerPrepares public-safe proof paths, limitations, and handoff wording.

Decision record handoff

Hand off the boundary, not a stronger answer.

The handoff should preserve the bounded question, static evidence state, validation evidence category, rule ID or rule family, proof path, evidence tier, coverage label, limitation, gap rows, next owners, and non-claims. Use the decision record when the meeting needs a durable result, and the handoff template when the next owner needs portable fields.

Stop conditions

Stop, downgrade, move private, or assign an owner when a boundary appears.

  • Stop when a proof path is missing or cannot be inspected publicly.
  • Stop when evidence is private-only, raw, source-like, configuration-like, credential-like, or not approved for public summary.
  • Stop when coverage is unknown or reduced without a visible label.
  • Stop before unsupported runtime, release, safety, production, endpoint performance, absence-of-impact, or complete-coverage wording.
  • Stop before AI analysis, LLM analysis, prompt classification, or governance-replacement wording.
  • Stop when no next owner is assigned for an unresolved gap.
  • Stop when wording blames a vendor, consultant, team, owner, reviewer, or codebase instead of naming the evidence state.

Non-claims

The agenda keeps static evidence separate from decisions it cannot make.

  • The agenda is not meeting automation, an approval workflow, release approval, release safety proof, operational safety proof, runtime proof, production traffic proof, endpoint performance proof, absence-of-impact proof, or complete coverage proof.
  • The agenda is not AI analysis, LLM analysis, embeddings, vector database reasoning, prompt-based classification, or automated impact analysis.
  • The agenda does not replace tests, code review, source review, runtime observability, release review, owner confirmation, human judgment, or governance.
  • Public notes should not include raw artifact material, local-only identifiers, private samples, hidden validation detail, credentials, or source snippets.
