Public demo troubleshooting

When demo guidance does not line up, keep the claim smaller.

This concept page helps a visitor route public demo confusion to the right public check, owner role, stop condition, and non-claim.

Public claim level: concept. No public conclusion without evidence. Missing, stale, private-only, reduced, unavailable, incomplete, or unsupported public signals stay visible until a public route resolves them.

Demo runbook Validation expectations Limitations and non-claims

When to use it

Use this when a public demo signal is confusing, stale, or incomplete.

Route confusionA public demo, proof, or adjacent route is missing, moved, or hard to connect to the claim.
Summary driftDemo summary wording appears older than the visible proof expectation or route metadata.
Proof mismatchThe page points to proof a public visitor cannot follow through public routes.
Coverage boundaryA reduced, partial, unavailable, or unknown label needs to remain visible.
Private-only materialEvidence may exist outside the public site but has no public summary yet.
Wording concernCopy needs downgrading, removal, validation context, or limitations context before it is repeated.

What it is not

This is guidance for reading the public demo, not a stronger proof surface.

  • Not a support contract, response-time promise, ticketing channel, or guaranteed answer path.
  • Not runtime diagnosis, production proof, endpoint performance proof, release approval, operational proof, or complete coverage.
  • Not an AI or LLM impact-analysis claim, prompt-based classification, embedding search, or vector database analysis.
  • Not a replacement for validation, tests, owner review, or human review.
  • Not a way to turn private or raw material into public proof without a public summary route.

Safe wording

Use phrasing that preserves labels, routes, and stop conditions.

Missing routeThe public route is missing or moved; wait for a resolving route or recorded deferral before repeating proof wording.
Stale summaryThe demo summary may be stale, so current-proof wording should wait.
Incomplete proofThe proof expectation is incomplete and needs a public route or summary.
Reduced coverageCoverage is reduced, so the label stays visible.
Private-only evidencePrivate-only evidence needs a public summary before it can support public copy.
Unsupported wordingThis wording should be downgraded, removed, or routed to limitations and validation.
Validation mismatchThe validation expectation does not match yet, so passed-validation wording should wait.
Owner handoffAsk the named owner for the next public check; the handoff is not a conclusion.

Rejected wording

Unsupported patterns may appear only as rejected examples.

  • The demo route is missing, so the claim is proven.
  • The summary is probably fine, so current release wording is safe.
  • Private-only evidence is enough public proof.
  • Reduced coverage proves no impact.
  • The proof route is confusing, but the release is approved.
  • The page diagnoses runtime behavior or endpoint performance.
  • TraceMap provides AI or LLM impact analysis.
  • The demo uses prompt-based classification, embedding search, or vector database analysis.
  • Troubleshooting replaces validation, tests, owner review, or human review.

Owner handoff

Hand off the evidence question without adding a service promise.

Demo ownerSymptom: demo summary or proof wording drift. Requested next check: public summary, proof route, and stop condition.
Site ownerSymptom: route, metadata, sitemap, or discovery confusion. Requested next check: public route status and bounded link text.
Validation ownerSymptom: validation expectation mismatch. Requested next check: public validation wording and route alignment.
ReviewerSymptom: unsupported wording. Requested next check: downgrade, remove, or route to limitations before repetition.

Stop conditions and non-claims

Stop when public evidence is missing, private-only, reduced, or mismatched.

  • Stop before repeating proof wording when the public route is missing, broken, or deferred.
  • Stop before current wording when the summary and proof expectation do not match.
  • Stop before strengthening coverage wording when a reduced, partial, unavailable, or unknown label is visible.
  • Stop before public proof wording when the only support is private-only or raw material.
  • Stop before passed-validation wording when validation expectations conflict.
  • These stops do not prove, approve, diagnose, certify, guarantee, or replace human review.
