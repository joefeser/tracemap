Site claim guardrails

Keep public TraceMap copy inside its proof boundary.

These guardrails help contributors, implementation agents, reviewers, and maintainers decide when site copy may repeat a claim, when it needs weaker wording, and when it must stay hidden.

Public claim level: concept. No public conclusion without evidence. This page is copy governance for deterministic static evidence, not a new scanner or reducer result.

Public claim levels

Claim levels govern wording strength, not product readiness.

shippedMain-true source behavior or source-document guidance has a public-safe proof path, rule ID or family, coverage label, limitation, and source context.
demoChecked-in public demo proof, sanctioned demo artifacts, or public-safe demo summaries support the exact wording and keep limitations visible.
conceptFuture-facing, explanatory, dev-only, process-level, or not-yet-backed wording stays framed as guidance and cannot be presented as shipped or demo-backed.
hiddenPrivate, unreleased, unsafe, or local-only detail is omitted or abstracted until an owner provides a public-safe summary.
Page-level boundaryThe concept label on this page does not upgrade any row into shipped behavior, demo-backed behavior, impact proof, runtime proof, release-safety proof, or complete coverage.

Proof-path requirements

A public claim needs all supporting fields before it grows stronger.

proof pathPoint to a public-safe route or artifact summary, with source context such as main, public demo, future-only, dev-only, hidden, or local-only.
rule basisUse a public-safe rule ID when available; otherwise use a rule family and a limitation explaining why the exact rule is unavailable.
evidence tierUse only Tier1Semantic, Tier2Structural, Tier3SyntaxOrTextual, or Tier4Unknown when a tier applies.
coverage labelTranscribe reduced, partial, unknown, unavailable, future-only, local-only, or hidden labels without normalizing them into stronger wording.
limitationNo claim is upgraded by confidence, seniority, repetition, urgency, roadmap intent, pressure, or appealing phrasing.

Allowed evidence references

Reference public-safe summaries and route families, not private proof.

Use public surfacesAllowed references include public pages, documentation, rule catalog pages, public-safe generated summaries, sanctioned demo artifacts, review packets, route metadata, coverage labels, limitations, and safe extractor-version labels.
Name artifact families only as categoriesFact streams, SQLite indexes, analyzer logs, reports, and rule catalog entries may be named as boundary categories, not linked as raw public proof.
Preserve the evidence boundaryKeep rule ID or family, evidence tier when applicable, coverage label, limitation, and source context beside the public statement.
Private-only supportPrivate-only evidence can support internal follow-up, but public copy needs a public-safe summary before it cites that support.

Forbidden raw material

Raw or private material stays out of public copy.

Do not publish raw proofForbidden categories include raw facts, raw SQLite databases, analyzer logs, source snippets, raw SQL, config values, secrets, local paths, remotes, generated scan directories, private sample names, command output, hidden validation details, and credential-like values.
Apply this everywhereThe boundary applies to rendered text, decoded HTML attributes, metadata, sitemap or discovery output, examples, fixtures, validation messages, and review-packet references.
Use abstract labelsHidden work is one abstract row or omitted; it does not reveal private names, internal route names, counts, cadence, sequencing, or private proof details.

Non-claim patterns

Say what static evidence does not establish.

Static boundaryTraceMap static evidence can show a bounded relation, gap, coverage label, or owner handoff without proving impact, absence of impact, safety, approval, business correctness, or production behavior.
Do not sayTraceMap proves runtime behavior, production traffic, endpoint performance, outage cause, release approval, release safety, operational safety, complete coverage, AI/LLM impact analysis, or replacement of human review.
Prefer neutral wordingUse phrases such as evidence shows, coverage is reduced, not established by this scan, needs review, owner follow-up needed, internal only, downgrade, hidden, and stop condition.

Validation expectations

Validation checks the route, not hidden proof.

Route and metadataThe standalone route needs concept-level title, description, canonical URL, Open Graph metadata, sitemap metadata, and discovery metadata.
StructureChecks cover visible concept level, the shared principle, required anchors, adjacent links, row markers, row fields, and body word-count bounds.
Public-safety sweepValidation scans rendered text, decoded attributes, raw HTML, metadata, sitemap, discovery output, tests, examples, and messages for forbidden claims or private/raw material.
Browser sanityDesktop and mobile sanity checks confirm the public page is readable and the table remains reachable without adding the route to primary navigation.

Review handoff

Use bounded next states when proof is missing.

repeat with proofThe public-safe proof path, rule basis, tier if applicable, coverage label, limitation, and source context are present.
downgrade before repeatingThe wording is stronger than the proof and must move to concept, demo, reduced, or narrower language.
owner follow-up neededAn owner must provide a missing proof path, public-safe summary, limitation, source context, or decision.
do not repeatThe sentence makes a forbidden implication or lacks the minimum proof fields.
internal onlyPrivate-only or raw-only evidence can guide internal follow-up but is not public proof.
hiddenPublishing details would expose private material, hidden work, or unsafe context.
