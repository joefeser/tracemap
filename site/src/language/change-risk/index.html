Change-risk language guide

Choose bounded words before repeating a change-risk claim.

This guide helps reviewers, managers, engineers, architects, and implementation agents describe deterministic static evidence without stretching it into runtime, safety, release, or impact conclusions.

Public claim level: concept. No public conclusion without evidence. The guide is wording discipline, not a product claim that TraceMap proves impact, absence of impact, safety, runtime behavior, or release readiness.

Why wording matters

Small wording upgrades can overstate static evidence.

Static evidence has a boundaryRule IDs, evidence tiers, proof paths, coverage labels, and limitations can support bounded review language.
Limitations are part of the claimA limitation travels in the sentence, not as an optional disclaimer after the conclusion.
Unknowns stay visibleReduced coverage, syntax fallback, missing adapters, and unclear ownership should become wording constraints or owner handoffs.
Adjacent pages keep their jobsThe review claim checklist decides repeatability; the claim review drill offers practice rows; this page chooses the words that keep evidence bounded.

Safe static-evidence phrases

Use words that keep the proof path attached.

Safe phrasing examples for public summaries.
Condition Allowed wording Evidence or boundary reason
Rule, tier, coverage, and limitation are present. Static evidence shows this source relationship under the cited rule. Keep the rule ID or rule family, evidence tier, proof path, and limitation beside the statement.
The evidence is useful but narrow. Evidence is limited to deterministic static analysis of the scanned source. Scope the claim to the available artifact family and coverage label.
Project load, semantic analysis, or an adapter did not fully run. Coverage is reduced; this scan still identifies review questions. Reduced coverage is not a clean result and not a reason to discard useful facts.
The name is noisy, high fan-out, or ownership is unclear. Needs review by the owner of the affected source area. Use review language when evidence exists but cannot carry a deterministic conclusion.
The next step is outside static evidence. Owner decision needed before stronger wording is repeated. Route runtime, release, business, or ownership conclusions to the accountable process.

Unsafe phrases

Treat these as blocked teaching examples, not product claims.

Unsafe or blocked phrasing that crosses the static-evidence boundary.
Condition Blocked wording Boundary reason
Static source evidence exists.TraceMap proved impact.Impact proof requires reducer-backed evidence and bounded wording; static clues alone do not prove impact.
A review packet looks clean.Safe to release.Release readiness and safety belong to release owners, tests, review, and runtime evidence.
No direct reference was found.No impact.Absence of impact is not established by a missing or reduced static signal.
A route or symbol is visible in source.Runtime confirms it.Runtime behavior needs runtime evidence and service-owner interpretation.
A production-facing name appears in source.Production is unaffected.Production behavior and traffic are outside static evidence.
A scan completed.Complete coverage.Coverage depends on tool load, adapters, evidence tiers, and documented gaps.
A reviewer summarizes the page.AI analyzed the change.TraceMap core scanner and reducer evidence is deterministic, not LLM or prompt classification.
A checklist row has no open gap.Approved for merge.Merge and release decisions remain human process decisions outside this guide.

Evidence-required wording

Use stronger words only when the supporting fields exist.

When to say evidence shows or TraceMap found.
Condition Allowed wording Evidence or boundary reason
A public-safe proof path, supporting ID, evidence tier, coverage label, and limitation are present. Evidence shows a static relationship under the cited rule. The statement remains tied to the proof path and does not drop the limitation.
The statement names a deterministic finding from static analysis. TraceMap found static evidence for this review question. This describes deterministic static evidence only, not runtime behavior, production behavior, release safety, or business correctness.
The requested conclusion needs runtime, operational, or release evidence. Not established by this scan. Do not upgrade static evidence into runtime behavior, production traffic, endpoint performance, operational safety, release safety, or complete coverage.
When to use needs review.
Condition Allowed wording Evidence or boundary reason
Evidence exists but does not support a deterministic conclusion.Needs review with the cited rule and limitation.Static evidence can frame the question without deciding it.
A name is common, noisy, or high fan-out.Needs review because the signal may match unrelated uses.High fan-out language prevents false certainty.
Ownership is unclear.Needs review by the likely source or service owner.Owner routing asks for a decision without assigning blame.
Coverage is reduced or expected evidence is missing.Needs review under reduced coverage.Reduced coverage limits the claim and keeps the gap visible.

Reduced-coverage wording

Label partial analysis without throwing away useful evidence.

When to say coverage is reduced.
Condition Allowed wording Evidence or boundary reason
MSBuild or project load failed.Coverage is reduced because project load did not complete.The scan can continue, but semantic conclusions may be unavailable.
Semantic analysis is unavailable.Coverage is reduced; evidence may be syntax or textual.Do not present fallback evidence as compiler-resolved proof.
Relevant adapters did not run.Coverage is reduced for that language or artifact family.The missing adapter constrains claims that depend on that coverage.
Expected evidence is missing.Coverage is reduced or the evidence is not established by this scan.Missing evidence is a gap, not proof of a clean change.

Owner-handoff wording

Ask for a decision without declaring the result.

owner decision neededUse when a reviewer must decide whether the static evidence changes review priority or next steps.
service owner review neededUse when runtime meaning, ownership, or service context is outside the scan.
release owner decision neededUse when the question asks for release timing, approval, or go/no-go process.
not established by this scanUse when absence of impact, runtime behavior, operational safety, or release readiness has not been proven.

Stop conditions

Stop before stronger wording when evidence or public-safety boundaries are missing.

When to stop and hand off instead of publishing stronger wording.
Condition Allowed wording Evidence or boundary reason
Required evidence is missing.Stop: owner decision needed before this claim is repeated.Do not fill missing evidence with confidence, seniority, or repetition.
Coverage is reduced and the claim depends on the missing coverage.Stop: coverage is reduced for this conclusion.Partial analysis can be useful, but it cannot support a stronger claim.
A reviewer asks for raw or private material.Stop: use a public-safe summary or keep the material private.Do not expose raw artifacts, private repository material, secrets, hidden validation detail, or command output.
Copy would imply release approval or safety.Stop: release owner decision needed.This guide does not approve, certify, or block releases.
Wording would blame a person, team, vendor, or implementation partner.Stop: describe the evidence gap and next owner instead.Public copy should stay neutral and evidence-centered.

Non-claims

This language guide does not upgrade static evidence.

  • It does not prove impact, prove absence of impact, approve releases, certify release safety, or establish operational safety.
  • It does not prove runtime behavior, production traffic, endpoint performance, business correctness, complete coverage, or replacement of human judgment.
  • It does not provide AI impact analysis, LLM analysis, embeddings, vector database analysis, prompt classification, autonomous review, or autonomous approval.
  • It does not publish raw facts, raw SQLite content, analyzer logs, raw source snippets, raw SQL, config values, secrets, local paths, raw remotes, generated scan directories, private sample names, raw command output, hidden validation details, or credential-like values.
